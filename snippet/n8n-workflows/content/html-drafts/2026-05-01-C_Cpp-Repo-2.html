The Breakdown

  • What it does: Drives all eight leg servos through a timed forward-walk cycle — setting each servo to a specific angle per gait frame, with a pressingCheck call between every frame that aborts the entire sequence if the forward input stops.
  • Key responsibility: Encodes the physical walking gait as a choreographed sequence of servo positions for Sesame's four legs — each call moves a specific joint, and the overall interleaved pattern implements a trotting stride that lifts diagonal leg pairs in alternation.
  • The clever part: Every pressingCheck fires between individual servo-angle assignments rather than between full cycles — so if the user releases the forward button mid-stride, the gait halts at the nearest frame boundary rather than finishing the cycle, keeping the robot's pose from landing in an unstable intermediate state.
